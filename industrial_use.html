How steel and cast iron are used in industrial setting?

Cast iron

Due to inferior strength and higher brittleness compared to steel, cast iron is not used for buildings and other structural uses. Despite this, cast iron is widely used in production of the cookware.

Cast iron is naturally grey in color but the cookware is black. It happens due to high carbon content, and when exposed to heat and oils, carbon layer appears on the surface which protects the cookware from rust and prevents food sticking. This process is called seasoning.

Steel

Due to high strength, steel is the main material structural parts of architecture are made. It is usually used with concrete to reinforce it.
